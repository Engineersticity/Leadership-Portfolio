Who is Erastus Kirui
A deep dive into my life

Background

My full name is Erastus Cheruiyot Kirui. I was born in the morning of June 14th, 1996 to Mr. David and Mrs. Florence Ngetich.I am a third born in a family of four children. I am proud of my family. Though it is not a very well to do family, we are happy.

My elementary life was chaotic, I had to learn the hard way. I had to customize my tears into a lesson, graft my sorrows into a step and take every bitter moment as a sentence to develop a dissertion. It took time yes but somehow I came to realize myself. I developed an inbuilt immunity to life chaos, I mutated into something close to an alien. Maybe worse radicalized not to give a damn to people’s opinions. I developed an attitude, centered my ecocentrism in my world of idealism. I had to be smart not by looking down upon humanity but realizing the world belonged to none. I chose to fight it my way. Amending my constitution and appending my mistakes.

I spent my primary school days in a school that my father and he Teacher Colleagues started to educate their own children. We had moved to a new place and the only available school was 4 kilometres away. One had to walk through a small forest to get there. It was not safe for young Cheri(as my parents and siblings used to call me bcak then) and my siblings to get there. So he built a school for us and i think that became my initial view of the world. Seeing the depths that my father was willing to go to make sure we had the best education became a motivation to me. I was always the best student throughout my primary education.

So I became the best student in my district at the Primary School final exams and as a result was admitted to the best public High School in the country, The Alliance High School. It was at Alliance that I grew uo and shaped my passions. Besides being in the company of an exposed and bright minds from allover the country, I was going through an evolution of my own. I could heve never imagined myself getting to Alliance but here I was. A village boy at the best high school in the country. I had to make it work. My family still being an inspiratiom to me. It was here that the leader in me was revealed. I became the President of the Electronics Cluba and the Volleyball team captain. These two roles have always been a benchmark with regards to who I grow to become a leader. Technology and team building.

After High School I was elected the Youth President for my County where I represented over 5000 youth and led a team of 21 peers. It was my first experience as a Leader out there in the world. I learnt critical values that leaders need to have, small things like the ability to listen to people and empathize. I was recognized as the most innovative Youth to have ever held that position. Later on in September 2014, I joined Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT) to Study Telecommunications and Information Engineering. This was after failing to go to a couple of universities abroad because I could not get full scholarship or my parents could not afford to provide a little that I was asked to top. I felt out of Place in JKUAT and started looking for other opportunities and that was how I ended up at the African Leadership University (ALU).

Foreground

I am a capable, creative and result‐oriented individual driven to develop Tech solutions that simplify lives of people by increasing efficiency and productivity whilst reducing costs. I study Computer Science (Software Engineering) at the African Leadership University, Rwanda Campus.

I am passionate about helping others and making this world a better place. I realize this as my cause but besides that I am interested in so many other things. I am curious to know how the world of artificial intelligence is going to do to our current social lives and day to day activities. I always research on new trends on mobile technology, internet and artificial intelligence. I have always loved computers and one of the core skills that I pride myself in is coding. I have been teaching myself this skill since when I came across computers. I am a web designer and developer and I have been practicing it as a freelancer. Coding gives me the thought of being in charge by creating a world in which I control everything. This is something that I have realized would make a person believe in themselves even more and even extend the control to their own lives and the people around them. Besides computers, I have learned to be a listener. I am proud to say that listening is one of the skills that have enabled me to do a lot in my life. The ability to communicate with people is another skill that has been essential to my leadership journey. Mr. Tunai, the governor I worked with, always said that if you can’t express yourself, then you are not supposed to be a human being. It never made sense back then, but now I fully understand where he was coming from. Beside all these, I have learned the hard way that respect is the boundary to all these values and it enables everything to fall in their place.

Entrepreneurship and design thinking are close to my heart as it is only by embracing this that we will be able to create more jobs for the unemployed youth of Africa and the world at large. I am dedicated to seeing more young people like myself take ownership of their lives and start more businesses across Africa. This will enable the world to move a step closer to the attainment of the sustainable development goals and address the unemployment problem, especially in the African continent. I believe Technology can take us there if fully embraced.

As the Head of Spin-offs, Tech Transfer and Entrepreneurship at Savi-Labs, I am in charge of ensuring that Technology related ideas and projects that come out of our Institutions of Higher Learning are nurtured and turned into business ventures that will create employment and increase economic growth. I am working towards developing a strategic long-term plan to work with African governments, African and Worldwide Civic Organizations to establish Technology Parks in specific centres in Africa through a clear and financially sustainable model that can be replicated anywhere on the African continent and elsewhere.

why the name 'Engineersticity'?

I am a very handy person. I love opening stuff, fixing them, modifying how they work and so forth. During my days at JKUAT, my friends would bring their calculators, laptops and even phones to me for fixing. So they started calling me Engineer, though we were all Engineers anyway. A while later, a crazy friend of mine, Noah Odondo, Started calling me 'Erasticity' because he wanted to give me a brand name. Combining the two names, I decided it was 'Engineersticity'

My Advice to the Youth In Africa

By 2050, Africa will have the largest workforce in the world. It is time that we think about this in a way that we have never thought before. All these people will want jobs and places to live and so on. As Fred Usually way, the greatest African Resource is above the ground. It is not the minerals and the valuable stones beneath our feet. It is the brains, talent and creativity that in the African people. Let us make use of our brains and create institutions that will ensure our children get the best education and life. The valuable stones will come later. We will use them to add value to ourselves, let us attach value to ourselves first.
